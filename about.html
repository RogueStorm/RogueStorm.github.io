A bit about me.
(It's not all about me!).

I was born in Crowborough, East Sussex, England, 11th November 1957. I attended two local schools, Sir Henry Fermor CofE Primary and The Crowborough Beacon Comprehensive. Like many, I didn't excel at school unless it involved sport. In November 1974, I joined the Royal Navy, liked it so stayed for twenty-three years. I became a software and database developer in 1997 and did that until 2013. I am a dad, grandad, full-time carer for my wife and part time writer. Dog lover, cats too if they’re sociable. I like goats, don’t read anything into that, and I’d love to help out at a farm during lambing but the logistics would conflict with my caring role. A keen photographer and I play golf when I can.

The Royal Navy
Nostris In Manibus Tuti

I was a very fresh faced, and naive teenager when I joined up, November 12th 1974. Seventeen years and one day. Training at HMS Ganges was followed by part two training at Culdrose in Cornwall. HMS Ark Royal and The BBC Sailor documentary was awaiting me. HMS Hermes for four years, including a trip to New Orleans for the Mardi Gras in 1980. That's a story in itsself. I sailed south for The Falklands in 1982, the MV Elk was the first civilian ship to sail. HMS Invincible next and a trip to Oz, New Zealand and Singapore, followed by six months in Dubai on RFA Dilligence and then a spell on the RFA Argus, a Flight Deck training ship. My shore time was predominantly at HMS Daedalus, Lee-On-Solent. My career finished with a role at RAF Scampton as part of a Joint Service Arms Control organisation (JACIG) responsible for inspecting ex-Soviet conventional weapons arsenals. My last official day in the Navy was 10th November 1997. I was a forty year old Chief Petty Officer and the following day I had to start paying for dental.

Software Development
Applications, Websites and Databases

I almost fell into software development by accident after I left the Navy. I specialised in Visual Basic, C#, SQL Server and a few other minor technologies. I worked for some interesting companies. Balfour Beaty Rail Renewal, responsible for train lines across the country, JMC Airlines, part of Thomas Cook, Glass' Car Guide, the car price people and lastly Airwave Solutions. They provide a secure TETRA radio network for the emergency services. Probably the most memorable aspect working for Airwave was that I got to work at Wembley Stadium during the London Olympics in 2012.

My Caring Role
It must be love.

I've been told many times that I do a great job looking after my wife. I've been asked quite a few times, how I cope. My reply always is, and always will be, the same. What is the other option?
   After Nem's stroke in 2011, I managed to keep working for a couple of years. Airwave had a work from home policy anyway which meant I could continue to work and look after her. Unfortunately, I was made redundant in 2013 and haven't had a full time, decent paying job since. Nem's condition over the last ten plus years has deteriorated to the point where she is practically bed ridden, needs help with feeding and drinking. She cannot speak, read or write and instructions are confusing for her (Global Aphasia). She also has dementia which, to my mind, makes life more bearable for her.

My Writing Career
What Career?

It was never my intention to be a writer. Some might think I'm still not. The story behind the book is documented on the Books page. This is just a brief timeline on how the process has gone:

  1. 2019 - I wrote a screenplay and submitted it to a competition.
  2. 2020 Jul - The screenplay peaked at the semi-final stage.
  3. 2020 Aug - I started to novelise the screenplay.
  4. 2021 Oct - Handed out the novel to twelve FB friends for evaluation.
  5. 2021 Nov - Started a 'Write That Book Master Class' organised by Michael Heppell.
  6. 2022 Jan - Began the audio compilation of the book.
  7. 2022 Feb - Submitted the manuscript to a publisher. Rejected.
  8. 2022 Mar - Completed the audio and the manuscript edits.
  9. 2022 Apr - Purchased ISBN numbers, barcodes and worked on my website.
  10. 2022 May - Submitted to Mixam printers and on 24/05/22, received the first copies of my printed book. Gave the book to some people for further evaluation.
  11. I've set 7th September 2020 as the full publication date. It will be our 37th wedding anniversary.
